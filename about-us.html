Vision

Why do we do what we do?

OUR VISION

To build such an educated society, where every citizen is ideologically, practically and financially self-sufficient.

All our efforts revolve around and indispensable guiding light i.e. our vision. It underlines our motive as a social enterprise and serves as the benchmark for assessing our approach. Our approach to meet the vision includes:

– To generate meaningful understanding of community

– To inspire their social inclusion

– To bring better services to lives of people

– To create income opportunities for the unskilled

Our Story

We literally started on the streets!

Our Story

It was in 2016, that we with the objective of giving back to the society, started mobilising rural people and providing value added education lessons such as Free Digital Literacy Educaion, Education Children, Oranizing Program for Rural People. Advertising was placed on Chawpals for additional outcome for the Rural People. The model soon became a phenomenon and started getting adopted across number of villages in the Khandwa District. This was just a beginning, as the socio-economic empowerment of the rural people was carried out through a number of initiatives ranging from financial inclusion to health services. The initiative was to systematize the working of Rural People by bringing them under one roof and modernize the sector with small, innovative and meaningful changes helping the rural people to learan digital lessons to increase their earnings. We have not only facilitated the upward mobility of the lives of thousands of Rural People but also pioneered the innovation in their lifestyle. Our efforts have brought about significant value additions to Govt. policies laid down for the unorganized working communities. Since then Parmarthi has been working with marginalized sections of the society with thrust on sustainable livelihoods, community mobilization and participatory management. In 2021 we integrated Parmarthi Pathshala in our corpus by launching Pathshala's with the support of rural people in five different villages of the Khandwa district which are lead by the rural women. We also started Parmarthi Gaushala in Takli Kalan of District Khandwa in2021 to keep all those cows which were useless and non productive from the society's prespective.
